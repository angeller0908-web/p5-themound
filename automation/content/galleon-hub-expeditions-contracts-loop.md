## Inside the Ship: The Galleon Loop

Before stepping foot into the cursed jungles of *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu*, your journey begins on a creaking wooden galleon. Serving as the team's mobile base of operations, this vessel is where players prepare for the grueling expeditions ahead. 

The core gameplay loop centers on signing contracts with the ship's Captain. These contracts dictate your objectives, outline what treasures you need to recover, and highlight the hazards waiting in the jungle. It is on the Galleon that you and your squad must distribute weapons, stock up on essential gear, and decide on a strategy before heading to the rowboats. 

As you complete runs and survive the horrors, you will be able to unlock new starting points along the coast, allowing your team to push deeper into unexplored territories and locate abandoned forts hidden within the foliage.

## Gearing Up and Managing Resources

Survival in *The Mound* is heavily dependent on preparation. Unlike arcade shooters, inventory space and resource distribution require careful coordination. 

- **Weapons and Utility Gear:** Players must share the ship's armory. Deciding who carries heavy firepower versus who handles utility items (like lanterns or sanity-restoring items) is key.
- **Contract Selection:** Different contracts offer varying rewards, but they also bring higher risks. Choosing the right contract depends on your team's current gear and confidence.
- **Unlocking the Coastline:** Discovering abandoned outposts and forts during runs unlocks permanent progression paths on the Galleon's map room, cutting down travel time for future expeditions.

## The Sanity System and Spatial Proximity Chat

One of the most intriguing features confirmed by developer ACE Team is the inclusion of spatialized proximity voice chat. In *The Mound*, communication is physical. If you wander too far from your teammates, their voices will fade into the ambient sounds of the jungle. 

This system ties directly into the game's sanity mechanics. As your sanity drops, the line between reality and illusion blurs. Community reports from the recent Steam Next Fest demo suggest that sanity depletion might trigger auditory hallucinations, potentially warping what you hear or causing you to misinterpret your teammates' locations.
