With the July 15, 2026 release date drawing near, players are sorting out their pre-orders for ACE Team and NACON’s upcoming cooperative survival-horror title, *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu*. For gamers operating in both console and PC ecosystems, one critical question remains: will the game support Xbox Play Anywhere?

Here is what we know so far about the cross-buy status, cross-save functionality, and how low-spec PC setups handle the game.

## The Xbox Play Anywhere Situation

Xbox Play Anywhere is a highly consumer-friendly program. When you buy a digital game supporting it, you own it on both Xbox Series X|S and Windows PC, with shared achievements and progress. 

For *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu*, early Microsoft Store listings showed the Xbox Play Anywhere tag. However, community reports on Reddit indicate some inconsistency, with the tag occasionally disappearing or failing to show on certain regional checkout pages. 

- **Official Status**: Expected / Not fully confirmed. 
- **The Outlook**: Given NACON's history with similar releases, the digital version is highly likely to support dual ownership across Xbox Series X|S and the Microsoft Store on PC, but we advise checking the "Capabilities" section of the store listing directly at launch before purchasing.

For more details on pricing and availability, check out our [Platforms and Price Overview](release-date-platforms-price.html).

## How Cross-Progression Works

If Play Anywhere is active at launch, your campaign progress, character unlocks, and items will carry over seamlessly. 

However, this ecosystem is locked to the Microsoft Network. This means:
- Progress **will sync** between Xbox Series X|S and the Windows Store version on PC.
- Progress **will not sync** if you purchase the game on Steam or PlayStation 5.

While your save files are confined to their respective ecosystems, the multiplayer portion is much more flexible. The developers have planned cross-network play, allowing Steam, Epic Games Store, PlayStation 5, and Xbox players to team up. You can read about how this works in our [Co-op and Crossplay Guide](co-op-crossplay-solo-explained.html).

## Can Integrated Graphics Run the Microsoft Store PC Version?

If you plan to utilize your Play Anywhere copy on a laptop, hardware capability is a major consideration. The game features thick volumetric fog, dark swamp foliage, and complex lighting that represents the sanity-draining atmosphere. 

During the Steam Next Fest, community testers experimented with the demo on low-end machines. Notably, tests run on an **Intel Iris Xe integrated GPU** (paired with an Intel Core i5-1135G7 CPU and 16 GB of RAM) showed that the game is bootable and playable, though with compromises.

If you are running similar integrated hardware:
- **Resolution Scaling**: You will likely need to lower the render resolution or use upscaling features to maintain stable frame rates.
- **Settings**: Keep shadow quality and volumetric effects on Low.
- **Expectation**: While it runs, do not expect console-like fluid performance without heavy optimization.
