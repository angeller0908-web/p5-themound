ACE Team’s upcoming Cthulhu-infused survival game is officially ready for pre-purchase on digital storefronts, bringing along a nice perk for subscribers. With a release locked in for July 15, 2026, the developer is incentivizing early console adopters with direct discounts and day-one gear. 

The console editions are now live on the PlayStation Store and Microsoft Store, offering a straightforward discount and an exclusive weapon set for those willing to commit early.

## Secure Your Subscriber Discount

If you are already paying for PlayStation Plus or Xbox Game Pass, you can save some cash on your digital purchase. Pre-ordering the game on the PlayStation Store or Microsoft Store yields a flat **10% discount** for active subscribers of these services. 

While the baseline price details can be found in our [release date, platforms, and price breakdown](release-date-platforms-price.html), this subscription perk makes digital console platforms a highly competitive choice for day-one players. 

## Pre-Order Bonus: Lost Explorers' Swords Pack

Commiting to a pre-order on either PlayStation 5 or Xbox Series X|S secures the **Lost Explorers' Swords Pack**. This bundle adds a set of bonus blades to your inventory at launch, giving you immediate melee options to carry into the game's hostile, sanity-draining jungle.

While firearms will likely be scarce and loud, reliable melee weapons could be crucial when dealing with the [confirmed creatures and horrors](creatures-confirmed-preview.html) lurking under the canopy. Keeping a blade handy might also help you conserve resources, preventing panic when the game's brutal [sanity system](sanity-system-explained.html) starts taking its toll.

## Launch Details and Crossplay Potential

The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu launches globally on **July 15, 2026**, for PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X|S. Getting a head start on your console purchase is particularly tempting if you plan to tackle the horrors in a group, as the game's [co-op and crossplay support](co-op-crossplay-solo-explained.html) ensures you won't have to face the dark alone, regardless of your friends' chosen platform.

For players interested in the narrative roots of this Mesoamerican nightmare, it is worth looking into [Lovecraft's original novella](lovecraft-novella-knyan-yoth-nkai.html) to understand the underground horrors of K'n-yan that inspired ACE Team's setting.
