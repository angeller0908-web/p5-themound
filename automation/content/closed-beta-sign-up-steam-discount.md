## ACE Team Opens the Jungle Gates Early

With *The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu* set to release on **July 15, 2026**, players do not have to wait until next month to experience its tense multiplayer horror. Developer ACE Team is hosting a closed beta playtest starting today, running through the weekend of June 5 to June 7, 2026. 

This playtest gives cooperative squads their first hands-on look at how the game handles sanity-draining environments. Known for their unorthodox world-building in games like *Clash: Artifacts of Chaos* and the *Zeno Clash* series, ACE Team seems to be bringing that same surreal energy to a 19th-century expedition.

For more details on what platforms will support the final release, check out our [release, platform, and pricing breakdown](release-date-platforms-price.html).

## How to Register for the Closed Beta

Sign-ups are currently open for the weekend playtest. Since this is a closed beta, player slots may be limited based on server capacity. 

- **Platform:** The playtest is running on PC via Steam.
- **How to Sign Up:** You can request access directly on the game's Steam store page or through official developer sign-up links distributed via community channels.
- **Co-op Focus:** The beta focuses on the core online co-op mode, allowing you to team up with friends to test the group dynamics under pressure.

If you are wondering how the co-op systems will function in the final release, read our guide on [co-op and crossplay features](co-op-crossplay-solo-explained.html).

## Steam Milestone Unlocks a Launch Discount

Alongside the playtest announcement, Steam news confirmed that the community has successfully unlocked the first wishlist milestone. Everyone who purchases the game at launch will receive a **10% discount**. 

According to the developers, the entry price into the jungle drops further as more players add the game to their wishlists. This is a welcome incentive for squads looking to jump in together on day one without paying full price.

For a broader look at previous testing phases and wishlist rewards, you can read our [wishlist and demo guide](beta-demo-wishlist-guide.html).

## Reality-Bending Sanity Mechanics

Recent hands-on previews from outlets like IGN have compared the game’s mental degradation effects to the classic *Eternal Darkness: Sanity’s Requiem*. Rather than just lowering a stat bar, losing sanity in *The Mound* forces players to second-guess what they see and hear. 

In a co-op setting, these auditory and visual hallucinations can lead to player paranoia, where teammates might view completely different realities. We have broken down how these psychological threats affect your survival in our analysis of [the sanity system](sanity-system-explained.html).

While the exact list of sanity effects remains under wrap (and we expect to see more variations during this weekend's playtest), the promise of meta-horror elements makes this one of the more unique co-op horror titles coming this summer.
