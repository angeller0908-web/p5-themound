The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu Closed Beta Registration & Gameplay Details

The Quick Answer

Beta DatesJune 5 - June 8, 2026

Keys Provided3 per selected player
Release DateJuly 15, 2026
PlatformsPC (Steam), PS5, Xbox Series X|S

With The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu launching on July 15 across PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S, developer ACE Team and publisher Nacon are letting players into the jungle a bit early.

A closed beta test is officially running from June 5 to June 8. If you want to test your luck against Lovecraftian horrors before opening your wallet, here is exactly what you need to know about securing a spot and what to expect from the gameplay loop.

How to Register for the Closed Beta

Registration is currently live and will close on June 8. You can sign up through the My Nacon portal.

The best part about this playtest is how Nacon is handling access. Because co-op is the core focus of the game, players who are selected won't just get a single code. If you make it in, you will receive three keys in total: one for your own account, and two extra codes to hand out to your squad.

Selection emails with access instructions are expected to go out shortly before the servers open on June 5.

What's Actually in the Demo?

Based on the recent unedited gameplay showcase presented by Art Director Edmundo Bordeu, this isn't your standard horde shooter. If you go in expecting to gun down waves of monsters, you are probably not going to make it to extraction.

The beta will let you test the core loop, which revolves around a few key pillars:

  • Extraction Mechanics: You enter the jungle, scavenge for loot, and coordinate an extraction. The margin for error is notoriously thin, and running away is often the right call.
  • Unique Creature Behaviors: The monsters you encounter aren't just running at you in a straight line. According to the devs, each creature has specific triggers and hunting behaviors. You can check out our early look at the confirmed creatures here.
  • The Sanity System: This is easily the most interesting mechanic shown so far. Fear and sanity are actual resources you have to manage. When your sanity drops, the environment starts actively lying to you.

The developers noted on Reddit that the sanity system affects players asymmetrically. That means if your sanity is critically low, you might start seeing monsters, hearing sounds, or taking paths that your teammates literally cannot see on their screens. You will have to constantly communicate to figure out what is real and what is a hallucination.

Looking Ahead to Launch

The wait won't be long even if you miss the beta window. The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu officially drops on July 15 for PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S.

We are still waiting on official PC system requirements and confirmation on whether the game will support full cross-platform play at launch. We'll update our release guide as soon as Nacon drops those details.

FAQ

How do I get a beta key for The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu?

You need to register via the My Nacon portal before June 8. If selected, the publisher will email you three keys—one for you and two for your friends.

Is The Mound a wave-based shooter?

No. The developers have clarified it is an extraction horror game. The goal is to enter the jungle, loot, manage your sanity, and extract alive. Surviving is prioritized over killing.

Will there be crossplay at launch?

While we know co-op is central to the experience, full crossplay functionality has not been officially confirmed yet.
