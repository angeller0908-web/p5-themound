How to Find Co-Op Players and Join LFG Groups for The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu

The Quick Answer

Release DateJuly 15, 2026
PlatformsPC (Steam, EGS), PS5, Xbox Series X|S
GenreCooperative Survival Horror
Max Lobby SizeExpected 4 players

With the July 15 launch rapidly approaching, the hype surrounding The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu is reaching global communities. Because this cooperative survival game pits players against brutal eldritch horrors beneath the earth, wandering into the dark alone is practically a death sentence.

If your regular gaming group is not into cosmic horror, you need to start looking for Looking For Group (LFG) communities. Here is how you can find like-minded explorers before the expedition begins.

The Rise of Global Co-Op Recruitments

Even before the official release, player groups around the world are organizing themselves. For instance, Portuguese-speaking survival fans on Reddit have already begun forming private recruitment circles on messaging apps like WhatsApp.

Since the title requires tight communication to balance resources and maintain mental stability, players are prioritizing pre-made lobbies over random public matchmaking.

Where to Find Teammates and LFG Hubs

Connecting with other players early will give you a head start when the game drops. We suggest keeping an eye on these primary channels:

  • The Steam Community Hub: The "Discussions" tab is already seeing LFG posts from solo players looking to build launch-day crews.
  • Dedicated Subreddits: Watch subreddits like /r/TheMound or general LFG subs where regional groups frequently advertise their private Discord links.
  • Discord Communities: While the official developer Discord will likely host the largest LFG rooms, smaller fan-managed servers often offer more tightly-knit teams.

Strategizing for the Underworld

Finding players is only the first step. To survive the depth of the Mound, your squad must coordinate roles. Based on early reveals, having teammates who understand the co-op and crossplay support details will make organizing lobbies across platforms much easier.

Moreover, you will need to prepare for the game's sanity mechanics. LFG groups that communicate via voice chat are expected to have a massive advantage, as coordinating who carries light sources and who monitors the team's mental decay will be crucial to avoiding the terrifying creature catalog lurking in the dark.

FAQ

Is matchmaking available in The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu?

Yes, the game is expected to feature standard public matchmaking, but finding a coordinated team via LFG groups is highly recommended due to the difficulty.

Where can I find regional players, such as Portuguese or Spanish speakers?

Look for regional recruitments on the Steam Community Hub, Reddit LFG threads, and community-run WhatsApp or Discord groups.
