The Anxiety of the Matchmaking Pool

With The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu scheduled to launch on July 15, 2026, console pre-orders have started popping up with early discounts (as reported by users on the PlayStation and Xbox stores). But along with the excitement of locking in their copies, players are already stressing over a fundamental question: How viable is this game if you are queuing up solo?

A recent community thread on the r/ACETeam subreddit highlights the dread many solo players feel when heading into tactical co-op games. Unlike casual shooters, tactical horror games require intense coordination. If you are matched with random players who refuse to communicate, drop out mid-game, or kick you right before the exit, the experience quickly turns into a nightmare.

Given what we know about the game’s complex sanity mechanics, playing with an uncoordinated squad could be a recipe for a fast wipe.

How ACE Team Might Handle Lobby Griefing

Co-op tactical games like GTFO and Lethal Company have shown that community-driven matchmaking can be incredibly volatile. (We have previously compared these dynamics in our comparative preview). For The Mound, developers ACE Team have not yet detailed their exact griefing countermeasures or lobby protection features.

However, community expectations and standard practices point to a few potential solutions: - Host-Kick Protections: Unconfirmed reports suggest the game might employ a vote-kick system rather than giving host players absolute power, reducing the risk of malicious late-game kicks. - Sanity Impact on Cooperation: In The Mound, your team's collective sanity affects the environment. A player who runs off alone or griefs the team will directly worsen the hallucinations for everyone, which ironically might force random lobbies to stick together out of sheer survival instinct. - Ping and Callout Wheel: Since voice chat with randoms can be hit-or-miss, a robust non-verbal communication tool is expected to be integrated, helping players coordinate without needing mics.

For those just starting out, checking our beginners guide will be crucial to understanding how to navigate these challenges even with a chaotic team.

The Buggy State of Early Playtests

Adding to the pre-launch discussions, some Italian content creators recently shared clips (captioned "Ci siamo buggati!" or "We got bugged!") showing unexpected physics glitches and collision issues during a private build test.

While bugs are a given for pre-release software, it serves as a reminder that The Mound’s complex, narrow cave systems are still being polished. If you plan to pre-order, you might want to review the console editions in our pre-order guide to see which package fits you best, or hold off to see how the launch-day patch addresses these collision bugs.

FAQ

Can you play The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu with random players?

Yes, matchmaking (solo queue) is expected to be supported at launch, allowing you to pair up with random players online.

Is a pre-order discount available for consoles?

Community reports suggest pre-order discounts are currently active on console storefronts ahead of the July 15, 2026 release.
