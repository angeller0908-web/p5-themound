With its launch looming on July 15, 2026, The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu has dropped a new trailer showcasing its atmospheric tension, monster designs, and sanity-altering mechanics. Developed by ACE Team—the Chilean studio widely recognized for the surreal worlds of Zeno Clash and The Eternal Cylinder—this upcoming horror title blends historical setting with the frantic tension of classic team-based survival.

The trailer outlines what players can expect from the cursed island, illustrating how sanity depletion changes the environments and distorts sensory information.

Left 4 Dead Meets 17th-Century Cthulhu

The core loop of The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu shifts away from modern military extraction setups in favor of a 17th-century expedition. Players form a four-person team equipped with period-appropriate tools, including crucifixes used to fend off eldritch forces.

The flow draws comparisons to Left 4 Dead, placing a heavy emphasis on positioning and resource sharing. To learn more about how to set up your squad across different systems, check out the details on co-op and crossplay mechanisms.

Stealth and knowing when to avoid combat altogether are critical. Rather than fighting through endless hordes, your primary goal is to locate legendary treasures hidden within the uncharted jungle and make it back to your extraction point before your group is overwhelmed by madness.

Managing the Unraveling Mind

The new footage highlights the sanity system, which serves as a major hazard during runs. As your expedition members spend time in the dark or encounter horrific anomalies, their minds begin to fracture. This results in: * Paranoia: Audio cues and visual distortions that make it difficult to determine real threats from imaginary ones. * Hallucinations: Expected to range from shifting geometry to phantom enemies that disappear upon taking damage (not fully confirmed). * Paralyzing Fear: Mechanics that slow down movement or restrict weapon handling if left untreated.

This psychological decay is closely tied to the game's setting, which takes inspiration from the subterranean horrors of H.P. Lovecraft's lore. Players familiar with K'n-yan, Yoth, and N'kai lore will recognize the ancient ruins and subterranean decay depicted in the trailer. For a detailed breakdown of how to manage these mental afflictions, refer to the sanity system overview.

ACE Team's Uncanny Creature Designs

ACE Team has a reputation for creating some of the most bizarre creature designs in gaming, and The Mound seems to benefit directly from this pedigree. The monsters shown in the trailer move away from typical Cthulhu tentacles to present more unsettling, asymmetrical forms.

From hulking brutes covered in fungal growths to agile stalkers that blend into the canopy, the creatures demand different tactical approaches. You can read up on the currently identified monsters in the creatures confirmed preview.

Xbox Achievements List Revealed

According to recent platform tracking reports, the Xbox Series X/S version of The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu will launch with 25 achievements. While the exact criteria for all 25 achievements remain under wraps, community speculation suggests they will reward successful extractions, finding rare relics, managing to survive at zero sanity, and defeating specific boss encounters hidden deep within the jungle ruins.

FAQ

Who is developing The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu?

The game is developed by ACE Team, the Chilean studio famous for surreal and unique titles like Zeno Clash, Rock of Ages, and The Eternal Cylinder.

What is the gameplay structure of the game?

It is a 17th-century four-player co-op extraction horror game. Players explore a cursed jungle to find treasure, manage sanity, and extract alive. For more on multiplayer, read the Co-op, Crossplay, and Solo guide.

Are there achievements to unlock on Xbox?

Yes, reports confirm that the Xbox Series X/S version features 25 achievements to unlock at launch.
