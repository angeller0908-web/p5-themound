Let's be straight with you up front: Nacon and ACE Team haven't published official PC requirements for The Mound yet. The Steam page is live for wishlisting, but the system requirements section hadn't been filled in with final numbers at the time of writing. Anything you see claiming to be "the" specs right now is an estimate — including ours. The difference is that we'll show our reasoning and clearly label what's a guess.

First, ignore the fake specs

If you've searched this already, you may have run into aggregator sites listing absurd "requirements" like a Core 2 Duo, 4 GB of RAM, and a GeForce 510. Those are auto-generated placeholders, and they're nonsense for a game like this. The Mound is releasing only on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S — there's no PS4 or Xbox One version. A game that skips last-gen consoles entirely is not going to run on a 15-year-old laptop GPU. Treat those listings as filler, not facts.

A quick tell for fake specs: if the "minimum" GPU predates the game by more than a console generation, someone's database filled in a default. Real requirements for a current-gen-only 2026 title start much higher.

Will it run on Steam Deck?

No official Steam Deck rating has been assigned yet, so we can't promise "Verified." That said, plenty of current-gen-only co-op horror games do run on the Deck at 30fps with tuned settings, so it's a reasonable hope rather than a guarantee. The bigger question mark for a game like this is text legibility on a small screen and whether the online co-op holds up on the go. We'll update this section once Valve issues a compatibility rating.

What about consoles?

On PS5 and Xbox Series X|S there's nothing to configure — the game is built for that hardware as its baseline, so you can expect a stable target framerate out of the box. If you're weighing platforms, remember the whole squad can play together regardless thanks to crossplay; we cover that in our co-op & crossplay guide.

When will official specs arrive?

Publishers usually finalize and post PC requirements in the weeks leading up to launch, so expect the real numbers to appear on the official Steam page sometime before July 15. Wishlist the game and you'll be in a good position to check the moment they go live. We're watching that page and will swap our estimates for the confirmed specs as soon as they're up.
